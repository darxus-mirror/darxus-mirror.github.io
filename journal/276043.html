Most of my concerns about the US federal government would be resolved by moving its power / control / money handling to the state level.

    The lower the level of government the more varied the competency. I would not want to be on the wrong side of a judicial election in upstate new york for example. For another fun example at the state level, look at what texas is doing with their schools in the past couple of weeks.

    At the federal level, once something stops being political it tends to be run reasonably and equitably if not the most efficiently. From what I've seen, if we could have health care that ran as poorly as medicare, it would still be far better than the average healthcare plan and less costly. The fact that our oldest (and presumably most expensive) 12% of our population which is covered by medicare are costing us less than 20% of healthcare spending (I really need better data for these numbers because I was using population graphs and spending graphs and integrating the numbers in my head but I tried to be conservative) seems to me to be a fairly good deal. I really wish the whole of the population could have service that good for that price.
